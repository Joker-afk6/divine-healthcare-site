Who we are

We actually show up for the people we work with.

Divine Home Health Care is based in Somerset, NJ. We work with adults who have developmental or physical disabilities, helping them learn, practice, and hold onto the skills they need to live as independently as possible.

The practical side of that looks like cooking, budgeting, getting around town, knowing how to handle a doctor's visit, and building real relationships in the community. The less obvious side is just being consistent. A lot of our clients have had programs come and go. We try to be the ones who stick.

"Our goal is simple: every person we support should be able to use the resources around them and feel like a full member of their community."

What we provide

Real support for real daily life.

Every service we offer is built around what the person actually needs, not a program checklist. Here's what that typically includes.

Personal care assistance

Support with bathing, grooming, dressing, and medication reminders for clients who need day-to-day physical help. Provided with consistency and real respect for dignity.

Community integration

We go out with our clients. Parks, bowling alleys, community events, and recreational centers. Getting off the couch and into the world matters, so we actually make it happen.

Daily living skills

Cooking, cleaning, budgeting, using public transit, navigating a grocery store. These can be learned or relearned. We work on them at a pace that sticks.

Communication training

Building vocabulary, practicing social situations, and learning how to use community resources. Some clients work on verbal communication. Others use alternative methods. We adapt to what works.

Behavioral support

When behavior gets in the way of daily life, we work on what's underneath it, not just the surface reaction. Strategies are built to hold over time, not just in session.

Physical wellness

Scheduled fitness time, walking trails, bowling leagues, gardening. Physical activity is part of the program because it genuinely makes a difference in how people feel and function.

Common questions

Things people usually ask us first.

Do you accept Medicaid?

Yes. Most of our services are covered through New Jersey Medicaid programs, including DDD-funded supports. We can walk you through what's available for your specific situation and help with the paperwork.

What areas do you serve?

We're based in Somerset, NJ and serve the surrounding counties. If you're not sure whether we cover your area, give us a call. It's a quick answer.

What if a client's needs change over time?

We revisit care plans regularly and adjust as things change. A plan that made sense at the start of the year may not make sense by the end. We expect it to shift.

How do you screen and train your staff?

Background checks, reference checks, and training specific to supporting adults with disabilities. We don't place someone with a client until we're confident they're a real match, not just available.

Do you work with families during the process?

Always. We stay in contact with family members throughout. You'll know how things are going, what we're working on, and where to reach us if something comes up.

How quickly can services start?

It depends on the situation, but we move as fast as the assessment process allows. We won't rush the fit to get someone placed quickly. Call us and we'll give you a realistic timeline.

Ready to talk? So are we.

Whether you're exploring options for yourself, a family member, or a client, we're happy to have a real conversation. No pressure, no pitch. Just an honest look at whether we're the right fit.
